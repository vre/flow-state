## Assassin's Teapot Mechanics

**TL;DR**: Teapot with two chambers dispenses different liquids by covering air holes that control flow via atmospheric pressure and surface tension.

### How It Works

**What**: Two internal chambers, each connected to spout and separate air hole on handle.

**Why**: Covering a hole blocks air entry to that chamber, preventing liquid flow.

**How**: Cover hole A → chamber B pours. Cover hole B → chamber A pours. Cover neither → both mix. Cover both → no flow.

**What Then**: Serve different liquids by controlling which holes you cover.

### Physics Principle

**What**: Surface tension prevents air bubbles sneaking past liquid through narrow spout.

**Why**: Atmospheric pressure holds liquid in sealed chambers without air entry path.

**How**: Air bubbling through spout increases water surface area, which surface tension opposes. Only path is through top holes.
