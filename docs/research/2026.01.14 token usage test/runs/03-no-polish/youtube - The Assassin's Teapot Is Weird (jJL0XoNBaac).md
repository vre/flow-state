## Summary

## How the Assassin's Teapot Works

**TL;DR**: The assassin's teapot pours three different drinks from separate chambers using air holes and surface tension.

### The Mechanism

**What**: Teapot with two chambers, each with a top hole, both leading to spout. Covering holes controls which liquid pours.

**Why**: Covering a hole prevents air entry, trapping liquid via atmospheric pressure.

**How**: Surface tension blocks air from bubbling through spout when hole is covered. Open hole = air enters, liquid flows. No holes covered = both pour (mixed), one covered = one pours, both covered = nothing pours.

## Hidden Gems

**Useful vs Unhelpful Pedantry**: Atmospheric pressure pushes liquid up straws (not suction pulls), but simplified models like "suction force" are acceptable if you understand limits (suction maxes at ~100 kPa atmospheric pressure).

## Comment Insights

**Key Takeaway**: Ancient Chinese assassination tool (241 BC) has practical modern uses - from coffee service to magic tricks.

**Historical Context**:
- **Origin**: Called 鸳鸯转香壶, first appeared Han Dynasty (241 BC), used by Queen Lü Zhi to poison emperor's mother; later by Song emperor Zhao Guangyi to kill Li Yu (@yiy149)

**Safety Tips**:
- **Pour order matters**: Always pour your own drink first - poison residue stays on spout, and top chamber liquid can drip into bottom chamber when vertical (@disturbedNeutrino, @Thedarkbunnyrabbit, @Krispymelon)
- **Design flaw**: Horizontal (side-by-side) chambers safer than vertical to prevent cross-contamination (@prairiedogs001)

**Technical Corrections**:
- **Straw mechanism**: Tongue creates vacuum by moving backward in mouth, not lungs - prevents drowning (@carlz0r)
- **Gradient mixing**: Partially covering holes creates mixed ratios, not just binary outputs (@jimjjewett)

**Practical Applications**:
- **Confirmed use**: TV shows used this for serving multiple drinks from one jug (@spangle)
- **Service efficiency**: Waiters could serve regular/decaf coffee or aircraft beverage service faster (@Sam_on_YouTube, @Adscam)
- **Design improvement**: Small poison chamber in regular teapot more practical than two full chambers (@Thundawich)
