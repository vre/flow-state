## Assassin's Teapot: How It Works

**TL;DR**: Two-chamber teapot uses finger placement over air holes to select which liquid pours via atmospheric pressure control.

### The Mechanism

**What**: Two chambers, each connected to spout and separate air holes on handle.

**Why**: Covering a hole prevents air pressure equalization in that chamber.

**How**: Covered chamber: no air enters top, surface tension blocks air through spout, 100 kPa atmospheric pressure traps liquid. Uncovered chamber: air enters top, liquid flows.

**What Then**: Pour blue, yellow, mixed, or nothing (both holes covered).
