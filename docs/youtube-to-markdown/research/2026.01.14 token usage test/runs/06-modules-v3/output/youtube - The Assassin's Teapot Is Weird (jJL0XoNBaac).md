## Summary

**TL;DR**: The assassin's teapot uses surface tension and atmospheric pressure to selectively pour different liquids from separate chambers by covering holes.

## The Assassin's Teapot Mechanism

**What**: Teapot with two chambers that pours three drinks by covering different handle holes.

**Why**: Atmospheric pressure holds liquid when holes are sealed, preventing air displacement.

**How**: Cover blue hole → blue liquid; yellow hole → yellow liquid; neither → both (green); both → nothing. Surface tension at spout prevents air bubbles.

**What Then**: Same principle in gauze-covered bottles and "inexhaustible bottle" magic trick.

## Hidden Gems

**Useful vs Unhelpful Pedantry**: Atmospheric pressure pushes liquid up straws (not suction). But like physicists saying "holes flow" in semiconductors, acceptable shortcuts exist when you understand constraints (100 kPa atmospheric limit, absolute zero).
