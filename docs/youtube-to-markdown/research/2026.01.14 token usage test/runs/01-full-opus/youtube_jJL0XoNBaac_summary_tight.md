## TL;DR
Assassin's teapot pours three drinks from one vessel by covering holes that control chamber airflow via surface tension.

## The Assassin's Teapot Mechanism

**What**: Two-chamber teapot selecting blue, yellow, or mixed drinks by covering top holes.

**Why**: Covering holes blocks air entry; atmospheric pressure stops flow (like finger on straw).

**How**: Cover blue hole → blue; yellow hole → yellow; neither → green mix; both → nothing. Surface tension prevents air bubbles at spout.

## Hidden Gems

### Suction Force Pedantry
Accept useful simplifications ("suction") despite technical inaccuracy (atmospheric pressure pushes, not pulls). Know limitations: 100 kPa ceiling.

### 2D Transparent Demonstration
Vertical chamber arrangement in clear acrylic shows mechanism with colored liquids.
