## Summary

**TL;DR**: Sourdough uses natural yeast (not commercial), requiring longer fermentation that breaks down wheat into complex acids - creating superior flavor, nutrition, and digestibility.

## Core Points

- **Definition**: Bread leavened with naturally occurring yeast from flour-water starter (maintained and fed regularly), not commercial yeast
- **History**: All bread was sourdough until ~100 years ago; commercial yeast enabled faster production but sacrifices proper wheat breakdown
- **Superiority**: Starter's bacteria + extended fermentation → complex acids, nuanced flavors (caramel, earthy notes), easier digestion
- **Ingredients**: Only flour, water, salt (enriched: +sugar, butter, eggs, milk)

## Fake Sourdough

Commercial "sourdough" often = yeasted bread + chemicals for tang. Check ingredients: no added yeast or preservatives. Make yourself or buy from artisan bakeries.

## Comment Insights

**Health/Digestive Benefits**:
- Multiple reports: reduced bloating, gluten-sensitive tolerating sourdough, weight loss
- Scientific focus: fructan breakdown, lactic acid production

**Geographic Divide**:
- Europeans (Italy, Poland, Slovakia, Austria, Norway): sourdough is default bread
- American-centric "artisan" framing doesn't reflect global reality

**Technical Pushback**:
- Long fermentation (24-48h) with commercial yeast produces comparable results
- Flour quality matters more than yeast source
- Key differentiator: lactic acid bacteria, not wild yeast alone

**Practical Barriers Debunked**:
- Starter lives in fridge for months unfed
- Batch-freezing works
- Hybrid methods (starter + commercial yeast) viable

**Historical Corrections**:
- Brewer's barm (yeast) widely used for centuries
- Unleavened bread was most common
- Egypt timeline error in video (claims 10,000 years ago)
